Weight categories

Underweight

  • Nutritional deficiencies: Insufficient intake of essential nutrients can weaken the immune system, lead to fatigue, and impair organ function.
  • Osteoporosis: Lower body weight can increase the risk of bone weakening and fractures.
  • Fertility issues: Women may face irregular menstrual cycles or difficulty conceiving due to low body weight.
  • Weakened immune system: Underweight individuals may be more susceptible to infections and illness due to a compromised immune system.

Overweight

  • Heart disease and stroke: Excess weight can raise blood pressure and cholesterol levels, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.
  • Type 2 diabetes: Obesity is a significant risk factor for developing insulin resistance and type 2 diabetes.
  • Joint problems: The additional weight puts strain on joints, leading to conditions like osteoarthritis.
  • Sleep apnea: Obesity increases the likelihood of experiencing interrupted breathing during sleep, leading to sleep apnea.

Obese

  • Higher risk of cancers: Obesity is associated with an increased risk of various cancers, including breast, colon, and pancreatic cancer.
  • Mental health issues: Obesity can contribute to depression, low self-esteem, and body image concerns.
  • Liver disease: Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) is more prevalent among obese individuals.
  • Respiratory problems: Breathing difficulties, including asthma and shortness of breath, are more common in obese individuals.
